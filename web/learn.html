Play chemistry on a real crypto web

In MOLGANG the game is the protocol. Every bond you build is a real Knit, every molecule a Fiber, and your classmates validate your chemistry by staking pulses to vote. Nothing here is bought or sold — what you earn is verified knowledge and reputation.

The idea

A player proposes a chemical bond — a claim like “water is H2O”. The peers at your table who know their chemistry vote on it. When they reach a Byzantine-fault-tolerant quorum, the bond is woven into the shared web as a permanent, content-addressed record. Wrong chemistry gets caught by peers who know the material — assessment is built into play.

No tokens, no NFTs. XP, levels, quests, badges and the certificate are reputation and woven knowledge only — never a tradable or monetary asset.

Knits & Fibers

🪢 Knit

A single validated claim — one bond, settled on the ledger. Forming a bond writes a real Knit.

🧬 Fiber

A confirmed molecule woven into your Braid: an immutable account-state commitment with its own content ID (CID).

🌐 The Web

Everyone’s Fibers together form the shared Knitweb — a peer-to-peer graph of voted chemical facts.

🕸 Peers

Other players (and, when you’re solo, seeded table-mates) who stake pulses to validate your work.

Pulses & Silk — the two resources

Your wallet shows two numbers. They do different jobs:

⚡ PLS — pulses

Your real on-ledger balance. Pulses are the stake: a peer puts PLS on the line to vote (see below). New players get a faucet grant to start. Integer-only — 1 PLS is indivisible in play.

🧵 silk — the thread

Local “thread” fuel you spin into a proposal. Each bond you propose costs 1 silk (SILK_PER_BOND). It’s the destination for that 🧵 counter: silk is what lets you make a claim. When your bond is confirmed the silk is returned — good chemistry is free; only unproven proposing drains the spool.

So the 🧵 10 next to your PLS is your proposing fuel. Spend it to propose; earn it back when peers confirm you. Run low and you must get bonds confirmed (or wait for the faucet) before proposing more — which keeps the web full of claims people actually stand behind.

Staking pulses via PoUW votes

This is the heart of the protocol. A vote isn’t a click — it’s a stake. Proof-of-Useful-Work means peers put pulses on the line to back their judgement.

If the quorum doesn’t confirm — wrong chemistry, or not enough peers — every staked pulse is refunded and no reward is paid. Staking makes an honest vote the profitable one: back real chemistry and you get your pulse back plus a reward; rubber-stamp nonsense and the quorum leaves you out.

The Steel Slag track ⛏

A mission layer on top of the tiers, tied to real materials recovery.

When steel is made in a basic-oxygen furnace, what’s left over — BOF steel slag — is mostly four oxides: CaO SiO₂ MgO Al₂O₃, fluxed with CaCO₃ limestone. Millions of tonnes are stockpiled — and they hold recoverable value (vanadium, iron, minerals for soil).

The Steel Slag track asks you to build that oxide matrix. Each slag oxide you get confirmed refines the slag a step further; complete the matrix and you’ve modelled the exact composition a real recovery process starts from. It’s the same chemistry the ChemField / SmartSlag work turns into measured, signed data — the game teaches the matrix; the field recovers the value.

The track runs in two stages: map the oxide matrix (CaO, SiO₂, MgO, Al₂O₃), then add the flux (CaCO₃). Complete both and you earn the ⛏ Slag Refiner badge — a reputation mark that you’ve modelled a real recovery feedstock.

Why it matters: learning CaO·SiO₂·MgO·Al₂O₃ is the first step to reasoning about leaching and enrichment — “what’s in the slag, and what can we get out?” Open the Steel Slag track in the lab to build the matrix.

Certificate & wallet

At Level 2 you can mint a Proof-of-Useful-Work certificate — a PDF that states your wallet address, the pulses you spent doing useful validation, and a summary of what you wove, anchored to OriginTrail for provenance. The public copy redacts your private key; a bearer export exists only on the command line, behind an explicit flag. Your wallet’s “evaluation” is simply that proof-of-useful-work: not what you hold, but what you did.
